Executive Summary

For the credit committee.

Build a merchant credit passport as the operating system for Dutch e-commerce SME lending. Combine consent-based data from bank, PSPs, webshops, marketplaces and accounting — feed it through interpretable scoring — and put a human credit officer at the decision point. Start small. Earn the data. Learn the portfolio.

What

A standardised, consent-based underwriting file that reconstructs revenue stability, refunds, chargebacks, platform concentration and cash-flow coverage from the data merchants already produce.

Why now

PSD2 makes bank data routine. Commercial APIs expose PSP, webshop and accounting data. Bookkeeper-first distribution is open in NL. Existing lenders still underwrite on one slice.

The ask

Pre-seed funding to originate the first 5–10 loans under manual policy, build the data substrate, and reach 70 loans of repayment history within 15 months — enough for Seed diligence.

Most lenders see one channel. E-commerce SMEs live across many.

Speed isn't the bottleneck — signal breadth is. A merchant running on Shopify, bol.com, Mollie and Adyen looks small to a bank and partial to any single PSP.

02 · Product

The Merchant Credit Passport.

A standardised, consent-based underwriting file. Built for a human credit officer, not a black box.

  • Consent-based — merchant explicitly connects each data source and can revoke at any time.
  • Reconstructs reality — revenue stability, settlement timing, refunds, chargebacks, platform concentration, supplier punctuality.
  • Interpretable — every feature and deduction is visible. No mystery score.
  • Human-in-the-loop — passport supports a credit officer; it does not replace one.
  • Honest about limits — Ledger improves the signal; it does not remove credit risk.

Input: a 54-feature cross-source vector across 10 source families, with windows applied per-signal (not a uniform 15×4 grid). Preprocessing: pessimistic defaults applied at the pipeline level, so a missing source cannot improve a profile. Target: a synthetic default proxy today; real repayment outcomes in production. Output is a calibrated P(default).

Shadow mode guarantee

The ML score is never used to make decisions in Phase 2. It is logged against actual repayment outcomes to build validation data. A credit officer cannot see the shadow score — it is only visible in the demo dashboard as an illustration of what Phase 3 will use.

The €25k cap is always enforced In the pilot it usually binds — most approvals are sized at the €25,000 Year-1 ceiling rather than the cashflow or GMV anchors.
Tenor rules
  • High seasonality amplitude → shorter tenor
  • Low liquidity buffer → shorter tenor
  • Cashflow instability → max 9 months
  • Clean profile (0 red gates) → up to 18 months eligible
Reconciliation checks
  • PSP payouts must match bank credits ± 5%
  • Webshop orders must match PSP captures
  • Accounting revenue must match GMV reconstruction
  • VAT payments must appear in bank history

              04 · Business

              A narrow wedge, a credible MVP, defensibility through operational learning.

              Where Ledger fits in the SME-lending landscape, how it makes money, and what the next 24 months look like.

              Business model · six panels

              Target customer

              Dutch e-commerce SMEs with €0.3M–€5M GMV, ≥2 years trading, sales fragmented across PSPs, marketplaces and webshops.

              Revenue model

              • Net interest margin on the portfolio
              • 2.0% origination fee at draw-down
              • Small late / early-repayment fees (capped)

              Initial product

              • Amortising business term loan
              • €10,000 – €150,000
              • 3 – 18 months
              • No asset collateral in the base product

              Why now

              PSD2 and consent-based data access make broader underwriting commercially viable. Bookkeeper-first distribution is open in NL.

              Why Ledger can start

              • Narrow customer wedge (NL e-commerce)
              • E-commerce-specific risk features
              • Bookkeeper referral channel
              • Front-end-heavy MVP buildable today

              What this is not

              Not a licensed bank. The MVP is not a real underwriting engine. No claim of instant approval, permanent moat, or removal of credit risk.

              Competitive landscape
              Competitor type Good at Limitation Ledger's wedge

              Risks · named honestly, mitigated explicitly
              Privacy

              Data privacy risk

              Sensitive merchant and personal data flows through the platform.

              MitigationConsent logging, easy revocation, minimisation by default.
              Tech

              API access risk

              Third-party APIs can break, throttle, or change pricing.

              MitigationStart with reliable integrations and approved exports.
              Fraud

              Synthetic merchant risk

              Fake merchants can fabricate revenue across one channel.

              MitigationCross-reconcile bank, PSP, marketplace, accounting.
              Credit

              Credit-loss risk

              SME lending defaults are real, especially in early portfolios.

              MitigationYear-1 tickets small; manual review on every file.
              Model

              Model governance risk

              Black-box models drift and cannot be defended to regulators.

              MitigationInterpretable rules first; any ML in shadow mode only.
              Funding

              Warehouse funding risk

              Originating beyond funded capacity is fatal.

              MitigationHard cap: never lend beyond committed warehouse.
